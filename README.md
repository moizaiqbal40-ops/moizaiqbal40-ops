## About Me

I'm a Computer Science undergraduate at **Superior University Lahore**, currently in my 4th semester, focused on building a strong foundation in **Java, Data Structures & Algorithms, and Software Engineering principles** while extending into **Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning**.

What sets my approach apart isn't just writing code — it's understanding *why* a solution works before I consider it finished. I spend as much time reasoning through time and space complexity, edge cases, and design trade-offs as I do implementing them. I learn fast, explain technical ideas clearly, and prefer building projects that solve problems I've actually observed rather than tutorials I've simply followed.

My long-term goal is to grow into a **Software Engineer at Google or a comparable FAANG company**, and in the nearer term, to earn an internship at a top software house in Lahore — Arbisoft, Systems Limited, Confiz, Tkxel, VentureDive, Devsinc, Educative, or Netsol — where I can contribute to real production systems while learning from experienced engineers.

## Learning Philosophy

> Depth over speed. Understanding over memorization. Consistency over intensity.

I treat every course, project, and problem as an opportunity to build durable understanding rather than surface-level familiarity. I'd rather spend an extra hour understanding *why* an algorithm works than move on having only memorized *that* it works — because that depth is what separates engineers who can adapt from those who can only repeat.

<br/>

## Engineering Principles

- **Correctness first, optimization second** — a fast wrong answer is still wrong
- **Readable code is maintainable code** — I write for the next person reading it, including future me
- **Test before you trust** — every function earns its place through verification
- **Small, intentional commits** — a clean Git history tells the story of clear thinking
